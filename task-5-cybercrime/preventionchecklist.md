# Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist for Students

1. **Verify UPI Requests:** Never enter your UPI PIN to *receive* money. Your PIN is only required when money is being deducted from your account.
2. **Avoid Unknown QR Codes:** Do not scan random QR codes sent via WhatsApp or email promising cashbacks or rewards; these are often linked to payment requests.
3. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Turn on 2FA for all your important accounts, including email, GitHub, and social media platforms.
4. **Use Strong, Unique Passwords:** Avoid using the same password across multiple sites. Use a password manager to keep track of complex credentials.
5. **Beware of Phishing Links:** Do not click on links in unsolicited emails or SMS messages claiming your account is blocked or a package delivery failed.
6. **Keep Software Updated:** Regularly update your laptop's operating system, browser, and antivirus software to patch security vulnerabilities.
7. **Limit Public Wi-Fi Use:** Avoid making financial transactions or logging into sensitive portals while connected to unsecured, public campus Wi-Fi networks.
8. **Check URLs Carefully:** Before entering login details, ensure the website URL is correct and starts with "https" (look for the padlock icon).

### Official Reporting Channels
If you or someone you know is a victim of a cybercrime, report it immediately:
* **National Cyber Crime Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
* **National Cyber Crime Helpline:** Dial **1930** (specifically critical for immediate financial/UPI fraud reporting).
