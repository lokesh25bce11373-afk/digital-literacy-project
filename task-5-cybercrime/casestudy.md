# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

**What the crime is:** UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud occurs when cybercriminals manipulate victims into authorizing money transfers from their bank accounts. Scammers often use social engineering, masquerading as buyers, sellers, or customer support representatives, to trick users into entering their secret UPI PIN.

**How it typically happens (Step-by-Step):**
1. **The Hook:** A student lists an old textbook or a used bicycle for sale on an online marketplace (like OLX or Facebook Marketplace).
2. **The Contact:** The scammer contacts the student, expressing urgent interest and agreeing to the asking price without negotiation.
3. **The Trick:** The scammer claims they cannot pay in cash and insists on sending the money immediately via Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm. 
4. **The Execution:** Instead of sending money, the scammer uses the "Request Money" feature on the UPI app. They send a malicious link or a QR code and tell the student, "Enter your UPI PIN to receive the payment."
5. **The Theft:** Because UPI PINs are only used to *send* money, not receive it, the moment the student enters their PIN, the funds are instantly deducted from their account and transferred to the scammer.

**Who is usually targeted:** College students, senior citizens, and frequent users of online marketplaces are prime targets. Students are especially vulnerable when paying exam fees, booking travel tickets, or buying/selling second-hand goods online.

**The Consequences:** Victims suffer immediate financial loss. Because UPI transactions are instant and often transferred through mule accounts, recovering the stolen funds is incredibly difficult and time-consuming, leading to significant financial stress for students.
