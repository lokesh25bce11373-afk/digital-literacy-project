 Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

**5 Do's for Responsible Social Media Use:**
1. **Do** audit your privacy settings regularly to control who can see your personal posts and information.
2. **Do** use platforms like LinkedIn and Twitter to network with professionals and share academic projects or coding milestones.
3. **Do** think before you post—assume that university faculty or future employers will eventually see what you put online.
4. **Do** give proper credit and cite sources when sharing artwork, articles, or code snippets created by others.
5. **Do** engage positively with peers by leaving constructive, supportive comments rather than participating in online arguments.

**5 Don'ts for Responsible Social Media Use:**
1. **Don't** post sensitive personal information, such as your college ID, phone number, or home address, on public forums.
2. **Don't** engage in cyberbullying, harassment, or the use of hate speech under any circumstances.
3. **Don't** post photos of your batchmates or friends without asking for their permission first.
4. **Don't** complain about professors, specific courses, or university administration on public feeds.
5. **Don't** click on unverified links sent via direct messages, even if they appear to come from a friend's account, as they may be compromised.
